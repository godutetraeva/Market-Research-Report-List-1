<p><p>The augmented reality mobile games market can be divided into two distinct types: geographical location-based and non-geographical location-based. </p><p>Geographical location-based games utilize real-world locations to enhance gameplay, allowing players to interact with their environment, often through GPS technology. Examples include games that encourage exploration or treasure hunting in specific areas.</p><p>Non-geographical location-based games, on the other hand, focus on virtual elements that are not tied to real-world locations. These games often emphasize imaginative worlds and scenarios, providing immersive experiences without geographical constraints.</p></p>
