<p><p>Microfluidic systems are categorized into three main types based on materials: glass, polymer, and silicon. Glass microfluidics offer exceptional optical properties and chemical resistance, making them ideal for precise analytical applications. Polymer microfluidics, made from materials like PDMS, are favored for their cost-effectiveness, ease of fabrication, and flexibility, suitable for various biomedical applications. Silicon microfluidics, known for their integration with electronic components, provide high precision and scalability, often used in sensor and diagnostic technologies. Each type caters to specific application needs.</p></p>
