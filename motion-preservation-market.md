<p><p>The motion preservation market encompasses three main types: cervical artificial discs, lumbar artificial discs, and interspine spacers. Cervical artificial discs are implanted in the neck to replace damaged discs, allowing for continued movement and function. Lumbar artificial discs serve a similar purpose in the lower back, providing stability while preserving motion. Interspine spacers are designed to maintain disc height and alleviate pressure on nerves while promoting natural spinal movement. Together, these innovations aim to enhance patient outcomes and quality of life.</p></p>
