<p><ul><li>Hollow Core</li><li>Solid Core</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Photonic Bandgap Fiber market is segmented into two primary types: hollow core and solid core fibers. Hollow core fibers utilize a central void surrounded by a photonic bandgap structure, enabling light to travel with minimal loss, making them ideal for applications requiring high transmission efficiency and low latency. Solid core fibers, on the other hand, use a solid dielectric material to guide light. They are typically more robust and commonly used in telecommunications and data transmission, offering versatility in diverse applications.</p></p>
