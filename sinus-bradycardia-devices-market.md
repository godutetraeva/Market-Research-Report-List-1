<p><p>The sinus bradycardia devices market includes several key types, primarily pacemakers and implantable cardioverter defibrillators (ICDs). Pacemakers are designed to regulate heart rhythm by delivering electrical impulses to stimulate the heartbeat, particularly in patients experiencing slow heart rates. Implantable cardioverter defibrillators, on the other hand, are used to monitor heart rhythms and deliver shocks if a life-threatening arrhythmia occurs. Both devices play crucial roles in managing bradycardia and preventing severe cardiac events, enhancing patient quality of life.</p></p>
