Vertical steam generators are typically compact, favoring space efficiency, making them suitable for smaller facilities or where height is limited. In contrast, horizontal steam generators are generally larger, providing better maintenance access and heat transfer efficiency. Each type serves specific operational requirements, with vertical units often used in pressurized water reactors while horizontal units are prevalent in various reactor designs for enhanced performance.</p></p>
