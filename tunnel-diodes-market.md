<p><p>Tunnel diodes are semi-conductor devices characterized by their unique ability to allow current to flow in both directions due to quantum tunneling. The market can be divided into two primary types based on doping: heavily doped N-type and heavily doped P-type. Heavily doped N-type tunnel diodes have an excess of electrons, enhancing conductivity, while heavily doped P-type diodes contain excess holes. Both types are crucial for applications in high-frequency oscillators, amplifiers, and switching devices, benefiting various electronic sectors.</p></p>
